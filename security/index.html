Security & Responsible Disclosure

Faro is a space for a child. Security isn't a feature here — it's the foundation. This page explains how the closed channel keeps a child safe, and how to report an issue if you find one.

This page covers Faro specifically. For the umbrella policy covering all KhassinX apps, see khassinx.com/security.

How Faro keeps a child safe

A closed channel — no strangers, ever

Faro is a walled garden. A child can only talk to the adults that a grown-up has verified and bonded to them, one by one. There is no discovery, no public profiles, no friend requests, no way for an unknown person to reach a child. The child's app cannot search for or message anyone outside its verified bonds.

Verified bonds

Every bond is set up by an adult and confirmed face to face. When pairing two devices, both screens show a short sequence of symbols that the adult and child read to each other — usually on a call. If they match, the bond is trusted. This defeats impersonation, even if someone intercepts the invitation link.

Sealed end to end

Every message, drawing, and memory is end-to-end encrypted with Apple's CryptoKit. Content is sealed on one device and only opens on the verified device on the other side. The other household cannot read a bond that isn't theirs — and neither can we. We hold no keys and store no copies on any server.

Report and block

Any adult can report or block a contact from inside Faro at any time. Because content is end-to-end encrypted, a report shares only what you choose to send — we never read your conversations. Reports help us keep the channel safe.

A gentle, on-device assistant

Faro's AI runs entirely on your device. It's built to be kid-safe: when a child raises a sensitive topic, it gently points them back to their trusted adult instead of answering. It never profiles a child and never sends a conversation off the device.

Reporting a vulnerability

Email: security@khassinx.com
Machine-readable disclosure pointer: /.well-known/security.txt (RFC 9116)

Please include: a brief description, reproduction steps, the impact you observed, and any tooling or accounts you used. Never include another person's real data or content.

Scope

  • faro.khassinx.com (this website)
  • The Faro adult app and the Faro child app on the Apple App Store
  • The Faro pairing, encryption, and sync flows

Out of scope

  • Third-party services (Apple App Store, iCloud, FaceTime, Apple Push) — please report to Apple directly
  • Volumetric attacks (DDoS, brute force) — not vulnerabilities
  • Reports generated solely by automated scanners without reproducible proof of impact
  • Theoretical issues without a demonstrable attack path
  • Email spoofing on subdomains where we explicitly publish SPF/DKIM/DMARC

Response targets

  • Acknowledgement: within five business days
  • Initial triage: within fourteen days
  • Coordinated disclosure timeline: agreed case by case, typically ninety days for non-critical, expedited for critical

Safe harbor

We will not pursue legal action against researchers acting in good faith — investigating, reporting, and respecting our scope rules. This includes accessing only data necessary to demonstrate the issue, never exfiltrating user content, and giving us reasonable time to remediate before public disclosure.

Recognition

We welcome responsible disclosure and want to make it worth your time. We do not currently run a monetary bug bounty, but we offer:

  • Public acknowledgement on this page (with your consent, in the form you prefer)
  • Direct communication with the engineering team handling the fix
  • A formal credit in our release notes when the fix ships

If you find a flaw that could expose a child's content or break the closed channel, we treat it as the highest priority — reach out and we will respond quickly.

What we ask you to avoid

  • Do not access, modify, or delete content belonging to other people
  • Do not perform tests that degrade the service for other users
  • Do not publicly disclose a vulnerability before we have had a reasonable chance to fix it
  • Do not test against real bonds or real children's content
